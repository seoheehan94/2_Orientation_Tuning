Letter of Information and Informed Consent

We are delighted to invite you to participant in the online research study outlined below. Your participation is entirely voluntary, and you can withdraw from the study at any time.

Purpose of the experiment: The purpose of the experiment is to learn more about how people process complex visual information from natural environment. You will be fully debriefed on the rationale underlying this experiment upon completion.

What you will be doing: You will make judgements based on the stimuli or answer questions, as instructed to you on the computer screen.

Time commitment & compensation: This experiment takes approximately 45 minutes to complete. You will receive 1 credit for your participation.

Risk & Benefits: There are no risks to you participating in this experiment. Your biggest benefit will be knowledge. At the end of the experiment, we will explain all of the ideas that went into making this study and provide some additional readings related to the study. If you are interested, let us know and we will send you a copy of what we write about this experiment when we are done. Send us an email at (seohee.han@mail.utoronto.ca) for more information.

Confidentiality, Participation and Withdrawal: No one will know that you participated in this experiment and the data that you produce from this experiment will never have your name attached to it. Everything that happens in this experiment will stay completely confidential. Ultimately, we aim to present the results of this experiment at conferences and publish them in a journal, magazine, or newspaper. If we do, you will never be mentioned. Should you decide to withdraw from the experiment, you will still receive course credit.

Contact: If you have any questions about this experiment, you may email us at seohee.han@mail.utoronto.ca. If you have questions about your rights as a participant, you may contact the Office of Research Ethics at ethics.review@utoronto.ca or 416-946-3273.
